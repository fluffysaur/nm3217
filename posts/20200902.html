On Heinz-sight

The Signs and Symbols of Heinz.

Posted by Tan Yi Jia on September 2, 2020

Slices, a campaign by Heinz, was published in December 2007 by advertising agency McCann Erickson, London, UK.

Signifier

A Heinz Tomato Ketchup bottle (as labelled) is made to look like a sliced-up tomato with a rounded bottom and leaves on top on a bright red background. This image is accompanied by the caption: "No one grows Ketchup like Heinz".

Signified

By making the ketchup bottle resemble a tomato, Heinz is implying that their ketchup is so fresh it's literally the tomato itself. To allow more people to understand the message, the caption is added to accompany the image. The bright red background also subtly emphasises on the freshness of the ketchup, mimicking the red colour of a fresh tomato.

Evaluation

Several different ketchup brands exist in stores, hence Heinz aimed to use this campaign to differentiate its ketchup from others by implying a guaranteed freshness for their tomato ketchup - a branding strategy as well. When a customer who has seen this advertisement is faced with different ketchup brands, they may consider buying Heinz if they are looking for good quality and/or fresh ketchup. This can be due to two reasons:

First, they may infer from the advertisement that Heinz ketchup is indeed fresh and thus choose Heinz over other brands.

Second, though they may not fully believe the advertisement, their subconscious mind may be influenced into associating Heinz with (fresh) Ketchup (it is worth noting that Ketchup and Heinz are intentionally capitalised so viewers will pay special attention to these two words).

As such, when someone who has never bought ketchup suddenly finds a need to buy ketchup, they may think of buying a bottle of Heinz ketchup (since in their minds, Ketchup = Heinz).
